"Accessibility is the practice of making your websites usable by as many people as possible. We traditionally think of this as being about people with disabilities, but the practice of making sites accessible also benefits other groups such as those using mobile devices, or those with slow network connections." [MDN](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Learn/Accessibility/What_is_accessibility)

Accessibility opens doors to a web where everyone can engage and build.

This [talk](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pcMYcjtWwVI) on the subject sparked my dedication to pursuing accessibility.

Marcy Sutton Todd lists the [reasons](https://web-accessibility-v3.vercel.app/topics/introduction/what-is-accessibility#reasons-to-overcome-access-debt) to overcome Accessibility debt

+ Civil rights.
+ Legal issues.
+ Pride in your craft.
+ Business cases.

Pride in my craft pulled me in, but now it's civil rights that drives me forward.
